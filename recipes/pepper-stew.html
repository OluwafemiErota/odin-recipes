Pepper Stew

Description

Obe ata dindin is a flavorful Nigerian stew made of peppers, tomatoes, and onions. It is often served with a variety of accompaniments, such as rice, yam, plantains, and more. This popular dish originated in southwestern Nigeria and is enjoyed all over the country. This Stew does not need to cost an arm and a leg. This Nigerian pepper stew may just be all you need to spruce up your menu.

Ingredients:

  • 4 Red Bell Peppers (Tatashe)
  • 500g Assorted meats Boiled Eggs (Optional)
  • 3/4 cup Palm Oil
  • 3/4 Cup Vegetable Oil
  • 1.5 Whole Onions
  • Scotch bonnet (Ata rodo)
  • 1/2 Cup Dried Prawns
  • 1/2 Chopped Onion
  • 4 Tablespoons Crayfish Powder
  • Chicken/ Beef Seasoning
  • 3 Tablespoons Locust Beans
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a pot, add your washed and cut-up assorted meat, season with onions, curry, thyme, salt, and fresh pepper, and allow to steam for 10 minutes.
  2. Next in a blender, put in washed and cut-up onion, shombo (remove the seeds), fresh pepper (ata rodo), and a little water then blend till smooth.
  3. In the same oil, (you might need to reduce it) add chopped onion, locust beans (very important), blended pepper mix, salt, seasoning, and crayfish powder. stir and fry for 10 minutes.
  4. In another pot set on medium heat, pour in palm oil and vegetable oil, allow to heat, then fry the cooked meat till it is well fried, then take out the meat.
  5. Next in the steaming neat, add water to cover the meat and cook till soft.once the meat is well cooked, drain and set aside
  6. Add boiled eggs, and fried meat, stir to combine, then add water or meat stock then cook for 10 minutes
  7. And Obe Ata Dindin is Ready!!!serve with any carbohydrate of your choice, even protein.
